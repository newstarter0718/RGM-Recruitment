8.4 Template T-09: Case Library Submission Form

Field

Content Required (Guidance)

Field 1: Market Context

Market income level, channel structure, category maturity, DDI range, key competitive context (3–5 sentences)

Field 2: Business Problem

Specific problem identified through diagnosis — problem type (brand/price/pack/execution), where the gap sits, quantified if possible (e.g., penetration gap, price vs. DDI delta)

Field 3: Why This Pack Type

Rationale for choosing the pack solution — why this format, size, price, and channel; what alternatives were considered and why rejected

Field 4: Analysis and Tools Used

List the analytical tools applied: conjoint, price elasticity, cannibalization model, 6-dimension scorecard, business case template. Include key data outputs where available.

Field 5: Final Pack Solution

Exact pack specification: format, size, EPP, RSP, channel(s), launch date

Field 6: Execution Approach

How the pack was commercialized: distribution drive, POSM, incentive mechanics, distributor briefing, timeline

Field 7: Results

Quantified outcomes (volume vs. plan, penetration delta, cannibalization rate, NSR impact, system GP). Mark as [ILLUSTRATIVE] if not from verified source data.

Field 8: Key Learnings

Top 3–5 learnings, written as principles applicable beyond this market (not just "what happened in this market")

Field 9: Transferability

Explicit assessment of which markets or contexts this case transfers to, and what the key enabling conditions are

8.5 Quick Reference: Key Formulas and Benchmarks

Formula / Benchmark

Definition / Standard

Affordability Index

Current Entry Pack Price ÷ Local Magic Price Point × 100. Target range: 80–110. Above 110 = affordability intervention required.

DDI Benchmark

Entry pack should represent 3–6% of Daily Disposable Income in most emerging markets. Recalibrate annually. Validate market-specific with Consumer Research.

Cannibalization Threshold

Standard threshold: <30% of new pack volume from existing packs. Market-specific thresholds may be tighter (e.g., high-margin adjacent pack) or looser (e.g., low-margin existing pack being replaced).

Trial Rate Target

≥15% of target non-buyer segment in pilot channel within pilot period (typically 60–90 days).

Repeat Purchase Rate Target

≥20% of trial buyers purchasing again within 4 weeks of first trial.

EPP Compliance Rate

≥85% of audited outlets selling at or below defined EPP at 30 days.

Numeric Distribution Target

≥60% of priority target outlets reached by Week 1; ≥80% by Week 4.

Business Case NPV Threshold

Positive NPV at Year 3 under 80% of base volume scenario.

GP Margin Floor

Market-specific (typically 28–35% GP per case for recruitment packs). Minimum must be confirmed with Finance before pack design approval.

Scale/Fix/Exit Trigger

SCALE: ≥80% of volume target at 90 days + all other thresholds met. FIX: 50–80%. EXIT: <50% after Fix cycle attempted.
