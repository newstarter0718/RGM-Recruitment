6.3 Case 1 — Taiwan: Mini CAN 200ml CVS Recruitment Pack

Case 1: Taiwan — Mini CAN CVS Recruitment

Market: Taiwan | Pack Type: Recruitment Pack — Single Serve Impulse

Field 1: Market Context

Taiwan is a mature, MT/CVS-dominant NARTD market. SSD category share of NARTD has remained broadly flat at 9.1–9.8% from 2020–2024. Category penetration among consumers under 35 is stalling, with sweetened tea over-indexing in the youth segment. CVS (7-Eleven, FamilyMart) is the primary recruitment battleground for on-the-go and away-from-home occasions among the 15–29 age group. 55% of ambient sweetened tea in CVS sells at or below NT$20.

Field 2: Business Problem

SSD penetration stagnant, especially among consumers under 35. Growth opportunities identified in Work/School, On-the-go, and Away-from-Home Meal occasions. Existing entry pack (Sleek CAN 330ml) priced above the NT$20 CVS Magic Price Point, with average price high relative to tea alternatives. No pack positioned to effectively recruit young, light SSD buyers in the CVS channel.

Field 3: Why This Pack Type

Mini CAN 200ml was selected as a portion-right, price-right entry format for the CVS channel: (a) smallest viable IC serve matching typical light buyer consumption on-the-go; (b) deliverable at NT$13.5–16 average price — at or below the NT$20 CVS Magic Price Point; (c) conjoint research confirmed 200ml as the perceived ideal portion for non-buyers (avg. ideal 275ml; 200ml clears the purchase threshold at target price). Three-phase evolution designed: Phase 1 (Q4 2025) Mini CAN 200ml as quick-win; Phase 2 (Q2 2026) PET 300ml as ideal on-the-go portion replacement.

Field 4: Analysis and Tools Used

Consumer conjoint study (15–29 y.o. non-/light SSD buyers): validated Mini CAN 200ml as preferred format at NT$13.5 average price with value share uplift vs. baseline. Price elasticity modelling: confirmed NT$13 price delivers +0.08pp value share uplift in Total buyer base and +0.02pp specifically among 15–29 target segment. P&L simulation: Swire GDC and customer margin modelled at NT$15 RSP. 2024 FY Mini CAN penetration already at 5.3% (+1.7pp), confirming existing demand signal.

Field 5: Final Pack Solution

200ml Mini CAN (Sleek format) at NT$15–16 RSP (NT$13.5 average CVS price including promotions). Positioned as the lowest entry price in the SSD CVS portfolio. Phase 2: PET 300ml at NT$22 RSP (NT$18 average) for food-pairing and on-the-go occasions — confirmed as ideal portion for food pairing by target consumers (avg. 298ml for food-pairing fit).

Field 6: Execution Approach

CVS-first distribution strategy with 7-Eleven as lead customer. Cold placement (CVS chilled bay) is non-negotiable — not ambient. POSM: in-cooler shelf strips and till-point communication. Incentive mechanic: introductory trade offer to secure facing. 3-phase portfolio evolution communicated to trade to build confidence in roadmap — not a one-off promotion. Sales force trained on Mini CAN occasion narrative (snack/leisure, portion control, youth).

Field 7: Results

Phase 1 (to Q4 2025): Mini CAN 200ml penetration reached 5.3% of CVS buyers within existing markets, with +1.7pp growth vs. prior period. Proposal targets NT$16 RSP as formal entry point to drive best value share and transaction uplift in 15–29 segment. Full national rollout results pending Phase 2 completion (Q2 2026). [FLAG: Post-launch full performance data not available in source materials reviewed; market RGM team to update Case Library with 6-month results.]

Field 8: Key Learnings

(1) EPP design must start from the competitive environment in the specific channel — 55% of ambient tea in CVS below NT$20 is the operative benchmark, not the general SSD price ladder. (2) Phase-based portfolio evolution (Interim → Quick Win → Ideal) gives the trade confidence and avoids the perception of a one-off tactical promotion. (3) Conjoint research to validate portion size is essential — "small pack" is not a sufficient brief; the ml must be consumer-validated.

Field 9: Transferability

High transferability to: (a) other markets with active CVS/convenience channels targeting young consumers (Thailand CVS, Hong Kong CVS, Singapore); (b) markets where ambient beverages compete at price parity with SSD in convenience channels. Key transfer condition: chilled CVS distribution infrastructure and consumer conjoint data for portion and price validation.

6.4 Case 2 — Vietnam: RGB Entry Pack Strategic Assessment

Case 2: Vietnam — RGB Affordable Entry Pack

Market: Vietnam | Pack Type: Recruitment Pack — Returnable Glass Bottle

Field 1: Market Context

Vietnam is a high-volume, GT-dominated emerging market. Daily Disposable Income approximately VND 160,000–180,000 (2025). The OBPPC current portfolio has a structural gap at 3–4% DDI — approximately VND 5,000–7,500 — where the market has no affordable IC entry pack below the 235ml CAN (VND 8,000–9,000) or 300ml PET (VND 6,000). DDI benchmark indicates the VND 5,000–7,000 range is the operative entry zone for low-income GT consumers. Per capita is targeted to grow to regional benchmarks; current recruitment coverage does not reach the full pricing spectrum.

Field 2: Business Problem

Recruitment is not covering the full pricing spectrum: VND 5,000–6,000 price point represents a genuine white space. PET 300ml at VND 6,000 is the cheapest current option in GT grocery, but with short shelf life, rural distribution limitations, and pack impression challenges. The market needs an affordable entry pack that can reach rural GT, deliver at or below VND 7,000, and be economically viable at the bottler level.

Field 3: Why This Pack Type

RGB (Returnable Glass Bottle) was assessed as the primary candidate because: (a) lower production cost per unit vs. new PET mold capex; (b) longer shelf life than PET — critical for rural GT distribution; (c) established consumer familiarity with RGB in Vietnam GT and E&D; (d) sustainability alignment (25% refillable bottle target by 2030); and (e) global case studies (Pakistan, India) demonstrate RGB profitability at scale in GT channels. ASSP PET (250ml) was assessed as an alternative: lower complexity but requires ASSP production technology and has short shelf life concerns for rural GT.

Field 4: Analysis and Tools Used

OBPPC current state mapping across all channels (Grocery, MT, E&D) with GP margins and index data. Volume conjoint simulation (Year 1): PET 250ml = 1.6–3.2 MMUC; PET 200ml = 1.7–3.6 MMUC; CAN 180ml = 2.0–4.2 MMUC. Value chain simulation across 5-year horizon (Bottler Assumptions + KO Assumptions). Cannibalization modelling: PET 250/200 cannibalize PET 300 (100% replacement assumption); CAN 180 cannibalize CAN 235. Project team: cross-OU (SCC, CCBV, OU) with Finance, Marketing, SC, and Commercial workstreams.

Field 5: Final Pack Solution

Final pack recommendation not confirmed as of mid-2023 source materials — multiple options under evaluation: 200ml RGB at VND 7,000–8,000; 250ml PET at VND 5,000 (ASSP technology required); 200ml PET at VND 5,000 (ASSP technology required). Supply chain readiness assessment and VC simulation to determine final recommendation. [FLAG: Final pack selection and launch outcome not available in source materials reviewed. Vietnam RGM team to update Case Library with final recommendation and results.]

Field 6: Execution Approach

Pilot proposed in urban and rural GT channels across North, Central, and South Vietnam. Supply chain complexity — production line configuration, bottle washer, glass seeding, crate logistics — requires phased SC build-out. Consumer habit rebuilding required for RGB (if RGB selected): trade education on handling and returns; consumer communication on refresh and value.

Field 7: Results

Project initiated April 2023. Final volume projection at project initiation: Y1 ~1.7 MMUC (starting point), Y5 targeting 16% of total entry pack volume (India benchmark). [FLAG: Post-2023 progress and launch results not confirmed in reviewed source materials. Vietnam RGM team to provide update.]

Field 8: Key Learnings

(1) RGB assessment requires a dedicated SC workstream — the commercial case cannot be evaluated independently of the production, logistics, and crate investment requirements. (2) Volume projections for a new pack type with channel overlap (PET and RGB in same channel) require explicit cannibalization modelling: what replaces what, in what timeframe. (3) DDI benchmark alignment between OU and bottler is critical to lock before the VC simulation — different DDI assumptions produce materially different volume projections.

Field 9: Transferability

RGB entry pack strategy is transferable to other emerging GT markets where: (a) RGB supply chain infrastructure exists or is investable; (b) consumer familiarity with RGB is present; (c) DDI benchmark indicates PET entry price is above affordable range. Relevant markets: Cambodia (GT), Bangladesh (GT), Sub-Saharan Africa. Not directly transferable to CVS or MT dominant markets without format adaptation.

6.5 Case 3 — India (INSWA): 1L PET Affordable Home Entry Pack

Case 3: India (INSWA) — 1L PET Home Affordability Pack

Market: India | Pack Type: Affordability Pack — At-Home Entry Pack

Field 1: Market Context

India is a large, grocery-dominated emerging market with at-home NARTD occasions 4x larger than AFH. Coca-Cola at-home share is approximately half of AFH share. KO overall household penetration had stalled at 20–26% (2018–2020). The lead entry home pack prior to this initiative was Sparkling 1.25L at INR 65 — representing 19% of Daily Disposable Income, which the RGM analysis confirmed was blocking home penetration expansion. Grocery represents 67% of FMCG universe; KO penetration in grocery was approximately 22% at pack level in 2019.

Field 2: Business Problem

At-home occasion volume is the largest untapped growth pool. KO household penetration stagnant. Existing entry home pack (1.25L at INR 65) priced above the affordability threshold for the mass grocery consumer. Distribution limited by price — only 33% ND for the 1.25L pack. Result: a large addressable consumer pool excluded by price.

Field 3: Why This Pack Type

1L PET at INR 50 was selected because: (a) INR 50 is the magic price point in FMCG for transactions above INR 40 (highest transaction density); (b) 1L = 4 x 250ml serves — ideal for average Indian household of 4; (c) downsizing from 1.25L with a price maintained at INR 50 created a consumer-perceived value gain vs. competitive 1.25L at same price; (d) one universal mold for all SSD brands allowed fast capex execution with no per-brand tooling; (e) conjoint and NSR 2.0 analysis confirmed ~40% transaction uplift in the home segment.

Field 4: Analysis and Tools Used

Consumer conjoint + RGM 2.0 analysis (transaction uplift modelling). Household panel: HH penetration tracking (Kantar). Nielsen RMS: market share and distribution tracking. Financial modelling: preform weight optimization (37g → 33g → 29.8g) to improve system economics at scale while maintaining INR 50 consumer price. Value chain analysis: matching profitability of 1.25L at INR 65 with scale-driven bottom line improvement.

Field 5: Final Pack Solution

1L PET Sparkling at INR 50 magic price point. One universal mold across SSD brands (reduced capex, faster execution). Case of 15 bottles (vs. 12 for 1.25L) to maintain case volume. Priority launch: North and East India (35% of NARTD market). Channel strategy: 1L at INR 50 leads in Traditional Trade (88% of business); 1.25L at INR 65 maintained in Modern Trade with consumer promo mechanics.

Field 6: Execution Approach

Distribution approach: penetrate home pack within existing KO outlets AND use the pack as the lead vehicle for expanding KO FMCG footprint into new grocery outlets. Focus markets Phase 1: North/East India. Network: 200,000 net new KO outlets added YTD as part of horizontal expansion program led by 1L. Shopper communication: drinking moments, marketing activation.

Field 7: Results

Volume: 2x growth in Entry Home Segment vs. overall KO CSP growth in focus markets. Market share: +1.6pp vs. YTD Sep 2019 in pack segment — achieved despite competitor promotional response (1.25L price-dropped to INR 50). Household penetration: 3x increase in Entry Home segment in Priority Launch markets vs. rest of India. Distribution: 45% ND achieved for pack segment (+4pp). 200,000 net new KO outlets added via 1L-led horizontal expansion.

Field 8: Key Learnings

(1) Downsizing with a maintained magic price point can outperform a simple price cut — the 1L at INR 50 was more successful than the 1.25L at INR 50 (competitive response), demonstrating that the right-size-at-right-price creates stronger consumer value perception. (2) One universal mold across all SSD brands is a critical operational insight: allows fast execution, minimizes capex, and enables portfolio-wide entry-price point architecture with a single production investment. (3) Distribution expansion and affordability are linked: the 1L was the vector for entering 200,000 new outlets that the 1.25L at INR 65 could not reach.

Field 9: Transferability

High transferability to any large emerging market with: (a) at-home occasion dominance; (b) stagnant HH penetration due to above-DDI entry home pack pricing; (c) magic price point at or near the INR 50 equivalent in local currency; (d) universal mold economics feasible in local production context. Directly applicable to: Vietnam (future AT-home pack development), Cambodia (if AT-home occasion grows), Bangladesh.

6.6 Case 4 — Spain / Europe: System-Wide Affordability Intervention

Case 4: Spain / Europe — Affordability Speed-to-Market

Market: Spain / Europe | Pack Type: Affordability Pack — System-Wide EPP Intervention

Field 1: Market Context

Spain is a developed, MT-dominant market in a high-inflation environment (2024–2025). The European system had developed a dual affordability-premiumization strategy: protecting heavy buyers with upsized value packs while recruiting medium and light buyers with single-serve and mini formats. The affordability pillar focused on four elements: Right Pack, Right Price & Promo, Right Activation Tools, and Right Display Principles — a simplified framework for consistent market execution across bottler territories.

Field 2: Business Problem

Consumer affordability pressure in Spain resulting from sustained food and beverage inflation. Risk of transaction loss as consumers traded down to private label or cheaper alternatives. Challenge: typical RGM pack and pricing planning cycles (6–12 months) were too slow to respond to rapidly deteriorating consumer affordability. The system needed to demonstrate that it could execute an affordability response at commercial speed.

Field 3: Why This Pack Type

The response was not a single new pack — it was a system-wide repricing and pack-architecture reaffirmation: ensuring the right packs were at the right price in the right channels simultaneously. This included activating existing "duals" mechanics (retailer-exclusive pricing and bundle mechanics), reinforcing single-serve entry formats, and communicating value clearly in-store. The decision was made not to create new pack formats but to optimize the execution of the existing portfolio at EPP-aligned prices.

Field 4: Analysis and Tools Used

Shopper-centric heavy-medium-light (HML) consumer segmentation. Consumer pricing sensitivity research: light buyers driven by absolute entry price; heavy buyers drive by PPL vs. competitive alternatives. Marketing Mix Modelling (B-MAT framework) to identify optimal investment allocation across activation levers. Bottler alignment workshop to pre-align priorities before formal planning cycle.

Field 5: Final Pack Solution

System-wide affordability activation across the existing Spain portfolio, with EPP-anchored pricing enforced at outlet level. Key elements: (a) "Duals" strategy — large multipack format at value PPL for heavy buyers (protect volume); (b) single-serve ice-cold entry pack at EPP for light/medium buyer recruitment; (c) retailer-specific promotional mechanics negotiated to land right-price-right-pack in each channel. No new pack format required — execution of existing portfolio at correct price architecture.

Field 6: Execution Approach

Plan aligned October; launched January 2nd — 10-week planning and execution window. Success factors cited: (1) treated as the #1 commercial priority for the entire system (RGM, Marketing, Legal, Finance, Supply Chain, Bottler) from Day 1; (2) "progress over perfection" — launched with available materials, added new elements incrementally; (3) deep cross-functional collaboration allowed rapid decision-making; (4) bottler briefed and aligned before formal planning cycle opened.

Field 7: Results

Full quantitative results from Spain affordability intervention are not available in source materials reviewed. The case was cited in the RGMX @ Scale session (October 2025) as a reference model for commercial speed and cross-functional execution. [FLAG: RGM CoE Europe to provide quantified outcomes (NSR, volume, share, consumer metrics) for inclusion in next Playbook version.]

Field 8: Key Learnings

(1) Speed is a strategic capability: a 10-week plan-to-launch cycle in a developed market is achievable when the system makes it the #1 priority — not a side project. (2) Cross-functional ownership from Day 1 (not sequential sign-off) is the single most important governance change. Every function that can create a delay must be in the room from the start. (3) Progress over perfection: launching with 80% of the ideal execution plan and adding the remaining 20% in weeks 2–4 is superior to waiting for 100% readiness.

Field 9: Transferability

Very high. The commercial speed model and cross-functional ownership structure are directly transferable to any market facing a rapidly deteriorating affordability environment. The "Affordability Look of Success" framework (Right Pack / Right Price & Promo / Right Activation Tools / Right Display Principles) is a universal operating standard applicable across all SCC markets.

6.7 Case 5 — Pakistan: RGB and 1L PET Dual Recruitment Strategy

Case 5: Pakistan — RGB + 1L PET Dual Recruitment

Market: Pakistan | Pack Type: Recruitment + Affordability — Dual Entry Pack Strategy

Field 1: Market Context

Pakistan is an emerging market with the lowest consumer affordability among the Coca-Cola system's key benchmarked markets. Operating in a hyperinflationary environment (2022–2024): tea cup price rose PKR 30 → 50; bus ticket PKR 30 → 40; snacks PKR 25 → 40 — all reference price points for calibrating NARTD Magic Price Point. IC (immediate consumption) Entry Price Point targeted at PKR 30–50; FC (future consumption) maintained at Magic Price Point of PKR 100. Portfolio: RGB 250ml and 1L PET as dual recruitment anchors — both immediate and future consumption occasions.

Field 2: Business Problem

High inflation compressing consumer wallets and raising the risk of transaction loss at existing price points. IC entry price point drifting above affordability threshold for price-sensitive GT consumers. Risk of permanent exit by light buyers if not addressed. Simultaneous challenge: maintaining system profitability while offering accessible price points.

Field 3: Why This Pack Type

Dual-pack strategy rationale: (a) RGB 250ml = IC (immediate consumption) affordable entry at GT level, with strong brand presence and visibility; (b) 1L PET = FC (future consumption) at PKR 100 magic price point — at-home occasion and value buying. RGB: established consumer familiarity in Pakistan GT; refillable economics support margin at scale. 1L PET: ideal home pack for 4-person household (250ml serves × 4); PKR 100 maintained as magic price point while competitors were also adjusting upward.

Field 4: Analysis and Tools Used

Pricing Trinity model for RGB communication strategy. OBPPC workshop: system alignment on pack architecture, portfolio opportunities, and white spaces. Consumer focus groups with distributor engagement. KPI cascade: volume, numeric distribution, weighted distribution, transactions vs. LY. Asset investment analysis: 1M UC glass injection, 45,000 cooler doors, incremental customer margin investment on 1L and RGB.

Field 5: Final Pack Solution

IC: RGB 250ml — held IC entry price below food inflation index; glamorized brand experience through in-store communication and intrinsic pack assets. FC: 1L PET at PKR 100 magic price point — 2% reduction in price index vs. frequency packs, signaling value commitment. Case configuration maintained to ensure distributor handling economics viable.

Field 6: Execution Approach

Four pillars: (1) Pricing Excellence — Trinity-based RGB communication; IC entry below food inflation; FC magic price point. (2) Execution Excellence — volume and numeric KPIs with progressive slabs, rewards and recognition for distributors. (3) Consumer Focus — OBPPC workshop alignment; portfolio white space communication. (4) Collaborate Inside, Compete Outside — strong bottler-system joint planning. 50,000 outlet customer engagement program for RGB and 1L. Asset investment: +1M UC glass injection; +45,000 cooler doors.

Field 7: Results

Volume: +38% vs. LY overall volume and transactions. 1L PET: +40% vs. LY volume; +2% mix improvement. RGB: +147% vs. LY volume; sustained positive growth. Distribution: +20,000 outlets vs. LY. 1L ND: 70% vs. 61% LY; RGB ND: 48% vs. 41% LY. Value: +13% vs. LY. Share: maintained or gained vs. competition despite competitive promotional pressure. System profitability: steady margins maintained — evidencing that affordability and profitability are not mutually exclusive.

Field 8: Key Learnings

(1) IC and FC entry packs serve different consumer missions and must be managed with separate strategies — one price point does not address both occasions. (2) Asset investment (cooler doors + glass seeding) is often the binding constraint for RGB scale-up: the business case must include asset economics, not just pack-level economics. (3) The most powerful signal to the trade in a hyperinflationary environment is holding the FC magic price point while all other reference prices are rising — it creates a disproportionate brand salience effect at the point of purchase.

Field 9: Transferability

High transferability to: other high-inflation GT markets (Bangladesh, Sri Lanka, Sub-Saharan Africa). Dual IC/FC entry pack architecture (RGB + large PET) is particularly transferable. Key conditions: RGB SC infrastructure available; magic price point identified and defended; trade incentive mechanics feasible in distributor model.

6.8 Case 6 — Georgia (EMEA): Strategic Affordability SKU List

Case 6: Georgia (EMEA) — Affordability SKU List Governance

Market: Georgia | Pack Type: Affordability — Governance and Execution Model

Field 1: Market Context

Georgia is a small, competitive NARTD market with 3.7M population (declining from 5.4M in 1991). TCCS FY2023 SSD share: 61% (+4pp). IC mix at 42% (well above CCAR region average of 23%). Per cap in 2022 at 247 — highest in CCAR. Key competitive threats: local affordable FSSD brands with Soviet-era brand heritage; Pepsi and fragmented competitors. Inflation low by regional standards; competitive pressure the primary affordability driver rather than consumer income compression.

Field 2: Business Problem

Intense competition from local affordable brands with deep consumer loyalty and price advantage. TCCS faces challenge of maintaining affordability perception while growing at-home and at-venue volume. At-home volume constrained by lack of clearly defined affordable SKU list with consistent execution across Traditional Trade and Modern Trade.

Field 3: Why This Pack Type

Strategic decision: no price increase for the full portfolio in 2023 — focus on economy of scale and affordability governance. Key innovation was the Affordable SKU List (ASL): a defined set of entry and upsize packs (250ml, 1L for entry; 500ml, 2L for upsize) with committed pricing at the "create value for consumers" threshold. Not a new pack — a governance innovation that gave the sales force clear execution priorities and the trade a credible "no price increase" message.

Field 4: Analysis and Tools Used

Affordability SKU List development: selection of entry (250ml, 1L) and upsize (500ml, 2L) pack formats anchored to affordable price points. Competitive pricing analysis: water positioned strategically below market leader. PICOS development for ASL in-store execution. Pay-for-Performance incentive for TT and MT: two KPIs — availability (ASL penetration) and visibility (CDE per outlet and VPO).

Field 5: Final Pack Solution

Affordable SKU List: 250ml (IC entry) + 1L (FC entry) + 500ml (IC upsize) + 2L (FC upsize). Water innovation: positioned below Borjomi market leader to attract price-sensitive consumers and drive category growth. Channel differentiation: TT = PET; MT = PET and Glass; Horeca = Glass. New SKUs: 1L Sprite and 1L Fanta Tropic to broaden affordable multi-flavor footprint.

Field 6: Execution Approach

Sales force clarity: two metrics only — ASL availability (penetration of AFL) and visibility (CDE per outlet). Pay-for-Performance in both TT and MT aligned to these two KPIs. In-store: SOVI and SOCVI in-store exclusivity exchanges for outdoor branding. CDE investment: water-dedicated CDE doors to drive visible affordability signal at cold shelf.

Field 7: Results

ASL Numeric Penetration (TT+MT): 70% (+4pp vs. PY) → 76% (+8pp vs. PY). ASL VPO Volume vs. PY: +23% (TT+MT) → +64%. ASL Mix Volume (TT+MT): 65% (+2pp vs. PY). CDE Doors/Outlet vs. PY: +31%. Water Mix Volume: 9% (+2pp vs. PY). 1L Pack Volume Mix in SSD: 10% (+2pp vs. PY). FY2023 TCCS SSD share: 61% (+4pp).

Field 8: Key Learnings

(1) Affordability governance — defining an Affordable SKU List with two clear KPIs — is often more powerful than launching new packs. Clear priorities and a simple measurement framework outperformed complex trade programs in this market. (2) "No price increase" as a strategic commitment in a low-inflation environment is a powerful competitive tool — particularly against local competitors with heritage price positioning. (3) Channel-differentiated pack format (PET for TT, Glass for Horeca) allows affordability in volume channels while protecting brand equity in prestige channels.

Field 9: Transferability

High. The ASL governance model — define a small set of affordable packs, set two KPIs, pay-for-performance against those KPIs — is directly transferable to any market where the primary challenge is execution consistency rather than pack innovation. Particularly relevant for: Cambodia (consistent GT execution), Vietnam (off-invoice spend governance), Thailand (ASL for GT channel).

6.9 Case 7 — Cambodia: 580ml Coca-Cola Recruitment Pack Launch

Case 7: Cambodia — 580ml Coca-Cola PET GT + MT Entry Pack

Market: Cambodia | Pack Type: Recruitment — Frontier Emerging Market Entry Pack

Field 1: Market Context

Cambodia is a small frontier emerging market (population ~17M) with rapid urbanisation and growing modern trade penetration in Phnom Penh and provincial cities. GDP per capita ~USD 1,800 (2024). SSD category is price-sensitive and channel-fragmented: General Trade (GT) represents the dominant volume channel; Modern Trade (MT) is growing rapidly in Phnom Penh and Siem Reap. Key competitive dynamics: local fruit drink and water brands compete at KHR 500–800 price points; SSD entry price must be anchored below KHR 1,500 to remain accessible to the core rural and peri-urban consumer segment. Coca-Cola holds category leadership but penetration headroom is significant among rural households purchasing below monthly.

Field 2: Business Problem

Core Coca-Cola volume in Cambodia was concentrated in the 1.5L PET (at-home) and 330ml Can (MT/impulse) formats — neither accessible at the KHR 1,000–1,500 magic price point that represents the primary single-serve entry threshold in GT. This left a gap in the price-pack ladder: no Coca-Cola format was priced competitively enough for everyday single-serve consumption by rural consumers trading on daily cash income. The result: trial rates in rural GT remained low and category penetration among lower-income segments was stagnant.

Field 3: Why This Pack Type

The 580ml PET format was selected to address the rural GT single-serve occasion at the KHR 1,500 RSP. Analysis of the price-pack ladder confirmed that: (a) 1.5L PET was too large for single-serve and priced above the daily cash threshold; (b) 330ml Can was priced at KHR 2,000–2,500 in GT, above affordability ceiling; (c) the 580ml format, sized for one or two consumers at point of purchase, could be priced at KHR 1,500 MT and KHR 1,500 key GT — within the critical affordability window. Format size also aligned with the observed consumer behaviour of sharing a cold beverage between two people in rural GT settings.

Field 4: Analysis and Tools Used

6.10 Case 8 — Singapore: No Sugar Entry Pack Recruitment

Case 8: Singapore — Coca-Cola No Sugar 250ml PET CVS Entry Pack

Market: Singapore | Pack Type: Recruitment — No-Sugar Category Entry, CVS-Dominant

Field 1: Market Context

Singapore is a highly developed, CVS-dominant market (population ~6M; GDP per capita ~USD 88,000). The NARTD SSD category is mature: overall penetration is high, but volume growth has been flat-to-declining on Original Sugar variants due to government-led sugar reduction initiatives (Healthier Choice Programme; Nutri-Grade labelling effective 2023). Nutri-Grade legislation requires SSDs with >8g sugar per 100ml to display a "D" grade — the lowest — at point of sale in all channels. Coca-Cola Original at Nutri-Grade D faces a display disadvantage vs. no-sugar alternatives and healthier beverages at CVS checkout. No-Sugar SSD sub-category is growing at high single digits. Key consumer opportunity: younger consumers (18–35) who are willing to consider SSD but default to water or RTD tea due to sugar concern — a clear recruitment gap for No-Sugar Coca-Cola.

Field 2: Business Problem

Coca-Cola No Sugar existed in the Singapore portfolio in the 330ml can and 1.5L PET formats, but neither format was optimised for the CVS trial occasion. The 330ml can was priced at SGD 1.90–2.20 in CVS — above the entry price threshold for a consumer trialing no-sugar Coca-Cola for the first time (where expectations are benchmarked against water and RTD tea at SGD 1.20–1.60). The 1.5L was an at-home format with no relevance to the CVS impulse occasion. Penetration of No-Sugar among the 18–35 target segment was significantly below total SSD penetration — the format and price were not driving trial.

Field 3: Why This Pack Type

A 250ml PET format was identified as the optimal entry vehicle for the CVS single-serve occasion: (a) 250ml is portion-right for a single ice-cold consumption — not over-sized like 330ml for a consumer uncertain about the flavour of a no-sugar variant; (b) PET enables a lower RSP than can (production cost advantage at small volume); (c) at SGD 1.20–1.30 RSP in CVS, the 250ml PET sits within the RTD tea price band — directly competitive with the primary beverage alternative for the target segment; (d) the smaller size minimises consumer risk ("if I do not like it, I have only spent SGD 1.20"). Coca-Cola No Sugar 250ml PET at SGD 1.20 RSP in CVS. Channel focus: CVS only in Phase 1 (7-Eleven, Cheers, FairPrice Xpress). SKU clarity: No Sugar only in Phase 1 — no Original variant at this size, to maintain clear health positioning for the CVS younger consumer. POSM: shelf tag highlighting "No Sugar" and "SGD 1.20" — dual message targeting both the health-conscious and price-aware consumer. Cooler placement: eye-level in the cold zone alongside RTD tea competitors — not in the SSD section where the consumer has already made a brand decision.

Trade sell-in: 250ml PET positioned to CVS buyers as incremental to the 330ml Can — different occasion (trial vs. regular), different shelf location (health-adjacent vs. SSD bay), different consumer target (lapsed/new vs. existing buyer). Distributor briefing: clear RSP enforcement at SGD 1.20; no discounting permitted in first 90 days. In-store: cooler placement standard confirmed before launch — eye-level, cold, health zone. Digital activation: CVS app promotion for first 2 weeks; trial-purchase incentive (e.g., buy 2 earn 1 free) timed to drive initial basket penetration.
