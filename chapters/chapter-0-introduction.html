Chapter 0 Introduction

0.1 Purpose and Scope

This Playbook is the definitive operational and training reference for Recruitment Pack and Affordability Pack strategy across all Swire Coca-Cola (SCC) markets. It is designed to be picked up independently by any RGM practitioner at any level, in any market, without reliance on facilitator support.

The document covers the full commercial lifecycle: from initial market diagnosis through pack design, commercial deployment, performance measurement, and capability certification. It is structured to serve both as a practical working guide for day-to-day RGM decision-making and as the curriculum backbone for the five-stage Training and Certification Programme.

[PRINCIPLE] Recruitment and Affordability packs are not a universal solution. They are a targeted, evidence-based response to specific consumer and market conditions. This Playbook equips practitioners to determine when, where, and how to deploy them — and equally, when not to.

0.2 Who Should Use This Playbook

This Playbook is written for global applicability across all market income levels, trade structures, and channel configurations. Content is transferable between developed and emerging markets, modern trade and traditional trade, high-income and low-income consumer segments.

0.5 Key Definitions

Term

Definition

Recruitment Pack

A pack designed primarily to acquire new consumers into the brand or category by removing the trial barrier through an affordable entry price point, smaller serve size, or both. Success is measured by incremental transaction and penetration growth, not volume alone.

Affordability Pack

A pack designed to retain existing consumers whose purchasing power has been eroded by inflation or income pressure, by maintaining an accessible price point, typically through resizing or repricing an existing format.

OBPPC

Occasion — Brand — Pack — Price — Channel. The strategic framework linking pack decisions to consumer occasion, brand role, price architecture, and channel-specific execution requirements.

Entry Price Point (EPP)

The lowest affordable transaction price at which a consumer can engage with the brand or category, calibrated to a Magic Price Point in the local market.

Magic Price Point

A psychologically significant price threshold (e.g., IDR 3,000; INR 20; PKR 100) at which consumer transaction propensity increases disproportionately. Market-specific and inflation-sensitive.

Incremental Volume

Volume genuinely added to the system through new consumers or new occasions, not transferred from existing packs or channels. The primary measure of recruitment pack success.

Cannibalization

Volume transferred from existing, typically higher-margin packs to a new recruitment pack. Must be modelled and bounded before launch approval.

DDI

Daily Disposable Income. The primary affordability benchmark. A pack is considered affordable when its price represents 3–6% or less of local DDI (varies by market context).

CEP

Consumer Entry Point. The price or pack at which a new or lapsed consumer first engages with the brand or category in a given channel.

NSR

Net Sales Revenue. Total revenue after trade discounts, allowances, and applicable taxes. The primary revenue performance metric for pack initiatives.
