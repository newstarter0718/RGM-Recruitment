STAGE 1 QUESTION BANK — Foundation (20 MCQ)

Questions 1–5 above are Stage 1 sample questions. The following complete the Stage 1 bank to 20 questions.

Q6. The "Two-Engine Growth Model" in RGM refers to:

A. Volume growth and value growth as dual metrics

B. Recruitment (acquiring new consumers) and Retention (deepening value with existing consumers)

C. Modern trade growth and general trade growth as parallel channels

D. On-invoice discounts and off-invoice rebates as the two levers of trade spend

Correct Answer: B | Chapter: Ch.1

The Two-Engine Growth Model establishes that sustainable NARTD revenue growth requires two simultaneous strategies: recruitment (bringing new consumers into the brand/category) and retention (deepening frequency, occasion breadth, and premiumization with existing buyers). Neither engine is optional.

Q7. Under the RGMX framework (Revenue Growth & Margin Expansion), a Recruitment Pack is approved when:

A. It generates incremental volume regardless of its impact on system gross margin

B. It grows market share above the competitor's entry-price share position

C. It delivers both incremental volume AND demonstrates a credible path to positive system economics including the full value chain

D. It receives sign-off from the Country GM and Finance CFO simultaneously

Correct Answer: C | Chapter: Ch.1

Under RGMX, margin expansion is an explicit co-equal objective alongside revenue growth. A pack that grows volume but destroys system margin is not approved under RGMX. The business case must close at the system level — covering full value chain from production through customer margin to consumer price.

Q8. A Recruitment Pack that scores well on Consumer Fit and Channel Fit but has a GP% below the market threshold should:

A. Be approved with a 12-month monitoring condition

B. Be blocked at Economic Fit — any dimension scoring 1 is a blocking factor

C. Be submitted to Regional RGM for special approval given strong consumer metrics

D. Be launched in a limited pilot without a formal business case

Correct Answer: B | Chapter: Ch.3

Economic Fit score of 1 (GP% below threshold) is a blocking factor regardless of performance on other dimensions. No approval path exists for a pack that fails the minimum economic threshold. The correct action is redesign: adjust the pack format, cost structure, or EPP until the Economic Fit score clears the minimum.

Q9. The correct sequence in the OBPPC logic tree is:

A. Pack Format → Brand → Price → Channel → Consumer Insight → Execution

B. Consumer & Shopper Insight → Brand Role → Pack Role → Pack Format → Price Architecture → Channel → Execution → Measurement

C. Price Architecture → Consumer Insight → Pack Role → Brand Role → Channel → Execution

D. Channel → Consumer Insight → Pack Format → Price → Brand → Execution → Measurement

Correct Answer: B | Chapter: Ch.1

The OBPPC logic tree always starts with Consumer and Shopper Insight (occasion and need), flows through Brand Role, then Pack Role assignment, Pack Format, Price Architecture, Channel deployment, Execution Standards, and Performance Measurement. Starting from price or channel (Options C and D) is a common strategic error that produces pack designs disconnected from consumer insight.

Q10. A "Magic Price Point" is best defined as:

A. The lowest possible price at which a pack can be produced and still generate positive GP margin

B. A psychologically significant price threshold at which consumer transaction propensity increases disproportionately

C. The price at which 50% of target consumers indicate willingness to purchase in conjoint research

D. The price point at which the pack becomes the cheapest option in its channel and category

Correct Answer: B | Chapter: Ch.0 / Ch.2

A Magic Price Point is a market-specific, psychologically significant threshold (e.g., IDR 3,000; INR 20; PKR 100) where consumer purchasing behavior shifts disproportionately — driven by cognitive price anchors, cash denomination convenience, and category reference prices. It is not simply the cheapest price or the minimum margin price.

Q11. Which statement about cannibalization is CORRECT?

A. Cannibalization must be zero for a Recruitment Pack to be approved

B. A standard cannibalization threshold of <30% of new pack volume from existing packs applies, but market-specific thresholds may vary

C. Cannibalization is only relevant if the affected existing pack has a higher GP% than the new pack

D. Cannibalization is acceptable up to 50% of new pack volume in any context

Correct Answer: B | Chapter: Ch.0 / Ch.5

The standard benchmark threshold is <30% of new pack volume drawn from existing packs, but this must be calibrated to the specific market context — particularly if the adjacent pack has significantly higher margin. The key principle is that cannibalization is expected and must be modelled explicitly before launch; the threshold determines whether the business case remains positive net of cannibalization.

Q12. The primary difference between an "Entry Pack" and a "Frequency Pack" in OBPPC architecture is:

A. Entry Pack has a lower GP% by definition; Frequency Pack has a higher GP%

B. Entry Pack targets new/lapsed consumers by removing trial barriers; Frequency Pack drives repeat purchase by existing light-to-medium buyers

C. Entry Pack is always a smaller format than Frequency Pack

D. Entry Pack is sold only in General Trade; Frequency Pack is sold in Modern Trade

Correct Answer: B | Chapter: Ch.1

Pack role is defined by strategic intent and target consumer — not by size or channel. An Entry/Recruitment Pack targets consumer acquisition (non-buyers and lapsed buyers) by removing trial barriers through price or portion. A Frequency Pack targets repeat purchase behavior among existing lighter buyers. The same physical pack format could theoretically serve either role depending on its price point and channel deployment.

Q13. Which of the following best describes "Daily Disposable Income" (DDI) as used in RGM?

A. Total annual household income divided by 365 days

B. The amount a consumer has available to spend on discretionary FMCG items per day after fixed costs

C. The median daily income of the lowest income quintile in the target market

D. The amount required to purchase the brand's current entry pack

Correct Answer: B | Chapter: Ch.0 / Ch.2

DDI in the RGM context refers to the daily income available for discretionary spending — after fixed costs (rent, transport, utilities, staple food) — rather than total daily income. It is the operative affordability ceiling for impulse and NARTD purchases. The 3–6% of DDI benchmark for entry pack price refers to this discretionary portion, not total income.

Q14. The primary reason to "define exit criteria before launch" is:

A. To satisfy Finance requirements for budget approval

B. To prevent the most common governance failure: extending a failing pack beyond its diagnostic window and wasting trade investment

C. To enable the Regional RGM Lead to override a market decision if performance is below target

D. To satisfy the Stage 4 Training & Certification assessment requirement

Correct Answer: B | Chapter: Ch.1 / Ch.7

Pre-defining exit criteria prevents the most common governance failure: "launch and hope" without clear trigger points. Without pre-agreed criteria, teams consistently extend failing packs — consuming distribution space, trade investment, and management attention — rather than making a clear Fix or Exit decision. The criteria must be agreed before the pilot, not during or after.

Q15. A participant asks: "Should we launch a Recruitment Pack to address an Execution Problem (poor POSM compliance and distributor stockouts)?" The correct answer is:

A. Yes — a new pack with a lower price will drive trial regardless of execution issues

B. No — a new pack will fail for the same execution reasons as the existing pack; execution must be fixed first

C. Possibly — pilot the new pack in well-executed outlets first to determine if the problem is execution or pack

D. Yes — but only in Modern Trade where execution is more controllable

Correct Answer: B | Chapter: Ch.2

The Go/No-Go Decision Tree explicitly excludes execution problems from the pack solution path. A new pack deployed into a broken execution environment will fail for exactly the same reasons: wrong placement, wrong price, no POSM, stockouts. Launching a new pack to "restart" a category that is underperforming due to execution is a well-documented failure mode in the Coca-Cola system.

Q16. In the five-step diagnosis process, which step should ALWAYS be completed before channel diagnosis?

A. Problem Classification (Step 5)

B. Price-Pack Ladder Mapping (Step 3)

C. Affordability Context (Step 1) and Category & Brand Participation (Step 2)

D. Go/No-Go Decision (Step 5)

Correct Answer: C | Chapter: Ch.2

Steps 1 and 2 establish the macro affordability baseline (DDI, inflation, income segments) and the category/brand participation picture. These must be completed before Step 3 (price ladder) and Step 4 (channel diagnosis) because: (a) the affordability baseline calibrates what gap threshold is meaningful; and (b) brand participation data determines whether a price/pack gap actually translates to an incremental volume opportunity.

Q17. A market has DDI of approximately USD 6/day (urban, grocery consumer). Category DDI range for affordable single-serve IC pack = 3–5% DDI. What is the target EPP range?

A. USD 0.06 – 0.10

B. USD 0.18 – 0.30

C. USD 0.30 – 0.60

D. USD 0.60 – 1.20

Correct Answer: B | Chapter: Ch.2 / Ch.3

3% of USD 6 = USD 0.18; 5% of USD 6 = USD 0.30. Therefore the target EPP range is USD 0.18–0.30. This maps to the Cambodia-context KHR 750–1,250 range at prevailing exchange rates, consistent with the KHR 1,000 Magic Price Point identified for Cambodia GT channel.

Q18. The "Opportunity Heatmap" framework in Chapter 2 is used to:

A. Map competitor pack distribution by channel and segment

B. Summarize diagnostic findings across consumer segments and channels to identify priority areas for pack intervention

C. Track KPI performance over the first 90 days post-launch

D. Assign RACI responsibilities across functions for the launch plan

Correct Answer: B | Chapter: Ch.2

The Opportunity Heatmap is a diagnostic summary tool — not a performance tracking tool. It captures the relative size of identified gaps (High/Medium/Low) by consumer segment (non-buyer, light, medium, heavy) and channel, enabling the team to prioritize where to focus pack design and commercialization effort. It is completed at the end of the Market Diagnosis (Ch.2) and informs the Channel & Occasion targeting in Pack Design (Ch.3).

STAGE 2 — Data Interpretation Scenarios

Scenario 2-A: Market X Affordability Analysis

Market X Data: Category penetration 52%. Brand penetration 44%. Current entry pack: PET 330ml at USD 0.45. Local DDI: USD 8/day (urban). Magic Price Point for NARTD IC: USD 0.25. Competitive entry pack: local brand, 250ml PET at USD 0.25. Lapsed buyer rate: +12% YoY. Brand equity score: stable.

Question: Complete the Affordability Index calculation. Classify the problem type. State whether a pack intervention is warranted and why.

Model Answer: Affordability Index = USD 0.45 ÷ USD 0.25 × 100 = 180. This is well above the 110 threshold, confirming severe affordability stress. Brand penetration gap (52% category vs. 44% brand) is moderate; brand equity is stable — brand problem is ruled out. Rising lapsed buyer rate (+12%) with stable brand equity points to price pressure causing consumer exit. The competitive local brand at the Magic Price Point (USD 0.25) is winning on price in the IC entry segment. Classification: Price Problem. Pack intervention warranted: a new entry pack at or below USD 0.25 (3.1% of DDI) would address the EPP gap and directly compete with the local brand entry. Proceed to Step 3: Price-Pack Ladder Mapping to design the pack. Grade rubric: 30% for correct AI calculation; 30% for correct problem classification; 40% for quality of recommendation logic.

STAGE 3 QUESTION BANK — Pack Design & Business Case

Q19. In building a business case, which of the following is the correct volume estimation approach for a Recruitment Pack?

A. Total category volume × target market share gain

B. Year 1 volume = Penetration uplift target (number of new households) × Average purchase frequency × Average pack size

C. Competitor entry pack volume × your estimated distribution advantage

D. Category growth rate × number of target outlets

Correct Answer: B | Chapter: Ch.3

Recruitment pack volume must be built from first principles: (1) how many non-buyer or lapsed-buyer households will the pack reach through the pilot channel; (2) what % will trial (trial rate target); (3) what is the average purchase frequency in the pilot period. Estimating from total category volume or competitive share overstates the opportunity because it does not account for the specific constraints of reach, trial propensity, and distribution coverage.

Q20. A pack design starting from an existing 500ml format with a 10% price reduction is an example of:

A. Good pack design: using an existing format minimizes production cost and capex

B. A common design error: starting from the existing pack rather than from the target transaction price (EPP)

C. A Frequency Pack strategy, not a Recruitment Pack

D. An Affordability Pack approach, appropriate if the target is heavy buyer retention

Correct Answer: B | Chapter: Ch.3

Starting from an existing pack and applying a price reduction is explicitly identified as the most common pack design error in this Playbook. It generates affordability but does not create genuine entry-point innovation. A Recruitment Pack requires starting from the EPP (DDI-anchored, Magic Price Point aligned) and working backward to the optimal format and size. A 10% price reduction on a 500ml pack typically does not reach the EPP threshold for non-buyers.

STAGE 4 QUESTION BANK — Commercialization & Execution

Q21. A distributor in Tier 2 markets has received the new entry pack but is pricing it 15% above the defined EPP "because the transport cost to these outlets is higher." What is the correct response?

A. Accept: transport economics in Tier 2 justify a premium over the defined EPP

B. Escalate to Country GM: pricing decisions require executive sign-off

C. Address immediately: EPP compliance is one of the Five Non-Negotiables; a 15% deviation destroys the recruitment mechanic for target consumers

D. Monitor: if stockout rate is below 5%, pricing deviation is not critical

Correct Answer: C | Chapter: Ch.4

EPP compliance is a Non-Negotiable. A 15% premium above EPP potentially takes the pack above the Magic Price Point — exactly the threshold at which trial propensity drops disproportionately. The recruitment mechanism only works if the consumer encounters the pack at or below the EPP. The correct response is immediate: diagnose the transport cost issue (consider a distributor subsidy or revised case configuration for Tier 2) and enforce RSP compliance through distributor briefing and trade audit.

Q22. The first 90-day launch window is significant because:

A. Finance requires a quarterly review cycle for budget tracking

B. Trial velocity is highest in the first 60 days — stockouts or execution gaps in this window permanently damage recruitment momentum

C. The 90-day window aligns with the market annual planning cycle

D. Consumer research is not valid beyond 90 days after launch

Correct Answer: B | Chapter: Ch.4

Trial velocity is highest in the first 30–60 days when novelty, POSM visibility, and distributor incentive mechanics are active. A stockout in Week 3–4 — when consumers who tried the pack in Week 1–2 want to repeat purchase — is the single most damaging execution event in the launch window. This is why the 90-day window has maximum 3-day stockout tolerance and why the replenishment cycle must be confirmed before launch.

STAGE 5 — Certification Market Case Brief

Bangladesh Market Case Brief — Stage 5 Certification Scenario

[ILLUSTRATIVE CASE — for training purposes only. Bangladesh is used as the Stage 5 certification scenario because it does not appear as a primary case in Chapter 6, and represents a context (large, GT-dominated emerging market; high inflation; low per capita; traditional trade dominant) that tests the full end-to-end capability.]

Brief Element

Details

Market

Bangladesh

Context

Population: 170M. GDP per capita: ~USD 2,800 (2025). DDI (urban, grocery consumer): approximately BDT 400–600/day (USD 3.60–5.40). Food inflation 2024–2025: 9–11% annualized. NARTD category growing but from a low base. Traditional trade (GT) represents ~90% of NARTD volume. Modern trade is growing but concentrated in Dhaka and Chittagong. Cold chain limited outside major cities.

Category Situation

SSD category penetration: 28% (national household). Brand penetration: 19%. Current entry pack: PET 250ml at BDT 20 (equivalent to approximately 4.5% DDI for urban consumer). Magic Price Point in GT: BDT 10–15 for IC beverages. Competitive entry: local sparkling brand at BDT 12 for 200ml. Lapsed buyer rate: +18% in last 12 months. Brand equity score: stable among buyers; low awareness among non-buyers.

Business Problem

Brand penetration of 19% vs. category penetration of 28% indicates a brand recruitment gap. The current entry pack at BDT 20 is above the BDT 10–15 Magic Price Point for the GT IC occasion, creating a price barrier in the primary recruitment channel. The competitive local brand at BDT 12 has been gaining share in GT among price-sensitive light buyers and non-buyers.

Your Task

As the RGM Lead for Bangladesh, produce a complete Recruitment Pack proposal covering: (1) Market Diagnosis (use the 5-step framework); (2) Pack Recommendation (apply 6-dimension scoring; specify format, size, EPP, RSP, channel priority); (3) Business Case (volume, margin, NPV — Year 1–3); (4) Launch Plan (pre-launch readiness, 90-day timeline, five non-negotiables, KPIs); (5) Governance (decision rights, stage gates, RACI highlights). Maximum 10 pages. Prepare to present and defend in 10–15 minutes with 10 minutes Q&A.

Panel Assessment Criteria

Knowledge (20%): correct application of framework terminology; DDI benchmarking; problem classification. Analytics (25%): diagnosis quality; volume build-up logic; cannibalization modelling. Pack Design & Business Case (25%): EPP derivation from DDI; 6-dimension scoring logic; economic defensibility. Execution Plan (20%): completeness of 90-day plan; specificity of KPIs; realistic scale/fix/exit triggers. Presentation Defense (10%): clear recommendation; ability to defend against challenges on volume projection and cannibalization assumptions.

Common Challenge Questions from Panel

(1) "Your volume projection assumes 15% trial rate. What evidence supports this in a market with no prior data?" (2) "The local competitor at BDT 12 has been in market for 3 years. How will you win when you are entering at BDT 15?" (3) "Your business case shows positive NPV at Year 3 but negative in Year 1. How do you defend the trade investment in Year 1 to the CFO?" (4) "What is your Exit trigger and how will you explain an Exit to the trade after 90 days?"
